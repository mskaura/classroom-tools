The Fur Trade Negotiator

New France, early 1700s. The fur trade is booming — beaver pelts are worth a fortune in Europe. But behind every trade deal is a complex negotiation with real consequences. Choose your perspective and navigate three critical decisions.

📋 Curriculum Connection — for Teachers
Subject: History Grade: 7 Strand: New France, 1713–1850 Expectation: B1.3

Expectation B1.3: Describe the nature of relationships between Indigenous peoples and European settlers during the fur trade era, including trade networks, alliances, and the impacts of European goods and practices.

How this tool addresses it: Students take the perspective of either an Indigenous leader or a French fur trader and navigate three negotiation decisions, each with real historical tradeoffs. The simulation reveals asymmetries of power, the role of diplomatic protocols, and the long-term consequences of trade goods and territorial agreements. The outcome screen links student choices to actual historical consequences.
